Where Rancho Cucamonga Arrests Go: SBSD Station and West Valley Detention Center

Law enforcement in Rancho Cucamonga is provided by the San Bernardino County Sheriff's Department (SBSD). After an arrest, defendants are initially processed at the SBSD Rancho Cucamonga Station, located at 8794 Lion St, Rancho Cucamonga, CA 91730 (phone: 909-477-2800). This is the patrol station and initial holding point for city arrests.

For defendants who will be held for arraignment or require extended custody, transfer to the West Valley Detention Center (WVDC) is standard. The WVDC — located at 9500 Etiwanda Ave, Rancho Cucamonga, CA 91739 (phone: 909-350-2476) — is San Bernardino County's largest jail facility. It is a modern, high-capacity institution that handles the majority of pre-trial felony detainees from the Rancho Cucamonga, Ontario, Fontana, and Upland corridor.

To find someone in San Bernardino County custody, use the county inmate locator at jimsnetil.shr.sbcounty.gov. Note that an inmate will not appear in the system until the booking process is fully completed — which can take several hours after the time of arrest.

The Booking Timeline — SBSD Station to West Valley Detention Center

Booking in San Bernardino County is a multi-step process. Here is what happens from the moment someone is arrested in Rancho Cucamonga:

Step 1
Arrest and Transport to SBSD Station
The arresting SBSD deputy places the defendant in custody and transports them to the Rancho Cucamonga Station at 8794 Lion St. The arrest report is completed — documenting charges, circumstances, and identifying details.
Step 2
Personal Property Inventory
All personal items are taken, catalogued, and secured. A receipt is issued to the defendant. Phone contact with family typically ends at this point.
Step 3
Fingerprinting and Warrant Check
Electronic fingerprints are submitted to state and federal databases. Any outstanding warrants from San Bernardino County or other jurisdictions are identified here. A warrant hold can delay release even after bail is posted on the current charges.
Step 4
Photograph
A booking photo is taken. This — along with all the preceding data — is entered into the SBSD booking system and eventually feeds the public inmate locator.
Step 5
Medical Screening
California requires a health screening at every booking. This covers injuries, medications, communicable diseases, and mental health needs. The WVDC intake staff performs a more thorough evaluation upon arrival if the defendant is transferred there.
Step 6
Bail Amount Set
Bail is set according to the San Bernardino County Bail Schedule, posted at sb-court.org. The schedule sets standard amounts for each charge classification. The actual bail at a hearing may differ from the schedule if a judge adjusts it based on the defendant's record and risk level. Arraignment must happen within 48 hours of arrest (excluding Sundays and court holidays) under California Penal Code § 825.
Step 7
Transfer to West Valley Detention Center
Felony defendants and those who cannot post bail at the station are transported to the WVDC at 9500 Etiwanda Ave. Transfer adds 2 to 6 hours to the total time before the bond can be posted at the receiving facility. Total time from arrest to bail eligibility at WVDC is typically 8 to 18 hours for felony cases.

San Bernardino County Bail Schedule — Sample Amounts

San Bernardino County sets bail amounts through its own schedule, published by the Superior Court of California, County of San Bernardino (available at sb-court.org). These amounts apply at booking and serve as the standard — a judge at arraignment can increase or decrease bail based on the specific facts of the case.

Here are representative bail amounts for charges commonly seen in the Rancho Cucamonga / Inland Empire area:

Charge Penal Code Bail Amount
Assault with deadly weapon (not firearm) PC 245(a)(1) $30,000
Assault with a firearm PC 245(a)(2) $50,000
Burglary (first degree — residential) PC 459 $50,000
Robbery PC 211 $100,000
DUI with injury VC 23153 $100,000
Possession for sale (controlled substance) HS 11351 $25,000
Grand theft (over $950) PC 487 $20,000

Because San Bernardino County uses its own schedule — separate from Los Angeles County — the amounts for the same charge can differ. The critical strategic point remains the same: posting bail before arraignment locks in the scheduled amount. Waiting carries the risk of a judge increasing it, particularly for defendants with prior records.

How a Bail Bond Works in Rancho Cucamonga

Under California Insurance Code § 1800.4, the standard bail bond premium is 10% of the total bail amount. For a $50,000 bail, you pay $5,000 to the bondsman — not $50,000 to the court. The bondsman posts the full bail as a surety bond guaranteeing the defendant's court appearances. The 10% premium is a non-refundable service fee earned when the bond is posted.

Frequently Asked Questions

Where is someone taken after an arrest in Rancho Cucamonga?

Initial processing is at the SBSD Rancho Cucamonga Station, 8794 Lion St, Rancho Cucamonga, CA 91730. Most defendants facing felony charges are then transferred to the West Valley Detention Center at 9500 Etiwanda Ave, Rancho Cucamonga, CA 91739 — San Bernardino County's main pre-trial jail. Call WVDC at (909) 350-2476 or use the county inmate locator at jimsnetil.shr.sbcounty.gov.

How long does the West Valley Detention Center booking process take?

Booking at WVDC is a high-volume process. Total time from arrest to bail eligibility — including transport from the station to WVDC — typically ranges from 8 to 18 hours for felony cases. The inmate will not appear in the public database until booking is fully processed, so an early search returning no results does not mean the person hasn't been arrested.

What does a bail bond cost in Rancho Cucamonga?

The standard California premium is 10% of the bail amount. For a $50,000 San Bernardino County bail, that's $5,000 upfront — not $50,000 in cash. Angels Bail Bonds offers payment plans and zero-down options for qualifying clients.

Can bail be posted at the SBSD Rancho Cucamonga Station before transfer to WVDC?

Yes — if the defendant is still at the station and bail has been set, a bond can be posted there. If they've been transferred to WVDC, the bond must be posted at WVDC. Angels Bail Bonds monitors every stage and posts wherever the defendant is held.

Is the bail bond premium refundable if the case is dismissed?

No. The 10% premium is a non-refundable service fee earned at the time the bond is posted. Case outcome — including dismissal — has no effect on the premium.
